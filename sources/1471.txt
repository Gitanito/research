Nigel Buckmaster Contributors                                                                                                                           Hauntings &amp; Apparitions                                                                                                                                                          Reciprocal Apparitions                

Gives examples of reciprocal apparitions (one which is experienced by both percipient&nbsp;and agent), and considers whether they are evidence of&nbsp;exteriorization of consciousness or of telepathy.                                           Beaulieu Abbey                

An&nbsp;abbey in southern England reputed to be haunted.                                           Borley Rectory                

Until its destruction by fire in 1939, Borley Rectory, a Victorian building in Essex in southern England, was the focus of persistent claims of poltergeist and haunting phenomena.&nbsp;                                           Cheltenham Ghost                

This 1892 report describes frequent sightings by multiple witnesses&nbsp;of an apparitional figure, a woman dressed in black,&nbsp;by members of a household in Cheltenham in the south-west of England.&nbsp;                                           Cock Lane Ghost                

Notorious 1762 haunting episode, in which poltergeist-type knockings and scratchings heard in a London house became the cause of heated public controversy.                                           Theories About Collective Apparitions                

Examines the arguments for viewing apparitions either&nbsp;as telepathic hallucinations&nbsp;or as objective entities.                                           Electromagnetism and Paranormal Phenomena                

Electromagnetism may be implicated in reports of seemingly paranormal phenomena, some researchers believe, and detection devices are hugely popular with ghosthunters.                                           Ghost Hunting                

'Ghost hunting’ is a popular term for the investigation of claims of site-based paranormal phenomena, such as ghosts and poltergeists.&nbsp;                                           Why Do Ghosts Wear Clothes?                

The question ‘Why do ghosts wear clothes’ is sometimes posed as a skeptical retort to&nbsp;reports of ghosts and apparitions.
