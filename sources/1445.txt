  Brahmavadin, The.
 A Monthly Religious and Philosophical Journal / The Monthly Magazine of Religion, Philosophy and Allied Subjects.
 That which exists is one: sages call it variously
 1895--1914  Semimonthly, monthly (from 1898)
 Triplicane, Madras, India.
 Editor: Alasinga Perumal (1865-1909).
 Publisher: Tripilicant (Madras), Thompson & Co. (New York).  Succeeded by: Vedanta Kesari (1914-present)
  1/1, September 1895-19/, March-April 1914.
  Rs. 4/ $2.00  a year.

  This was published by Alasinga Perumal after Swami Vivekananda had suggested the project to  him and it was devoted to Vedanta as envisioned by Vivekananda  It contained contributions by various swamis who sought to spread the word of Vedanta to the West, such as Sister Nivedita and Swami Abhedananda,  but also contained occasional articles on Islam, Christianity, Jainism, etc.  Noted in The Metaphysical Magazine, 1896,  "List of Advance Thought Publications," The New Cycle, March 1900, 159-60, and The Metaphysical Magazine, February 1901, 159, and in Notes and Queries, January 1900.    Issues:Brahmavadin V4 N1 - V4 NS 23-24 Oct 1898-Sep 1899
