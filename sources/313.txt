  Luz, Union y Verdad.
 Periodico Semanal de Estudios Filosoficos y Morales / Revista Espiritista Kardeciana.
 1914--1919?  Weekly, monthly then finally bimonthly
 Barcelona (Gracia), Spain.  Language:  Spanish.  Editor: Jacinto Esteva Marata.
 Succeeds: Luz del Porvenir and La Union Espiritista (merged 1900 to form Luz y Union); Revista Espiritista-->Revista de Estudios Psicologicos (merged 1902 to form Luz y Union: Revista de Estudios Psicol√≥gicos); Los albores de la verdad (1917)
  1914-1919(?)  Continued numbering of Luz y Union.

 This was the product of the merger of Luz y Union and the Theosophical journal Los Albores de la Verdad, a combination reflected in the journal's cover design, three loosely clad young women bearing flowers, a mirror and a lamp of truth, draped over a sphinx.  Oscar M. Garcia Rodriguez, Historia del Periodismo Espiritista en Espana; De Kardec Hasta la Guerra Civil Espanola (2006), online at http://www.ameporto.org/pt/entrevistas/oscar.pdf.  The final surviving issue, from 1919, combined two months, bearing witness to the labor strife tearing Spain at the time, the precursor to the Civil War, and may have been the last published.  BNE.
