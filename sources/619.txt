 Revue contemporaine des sciences occultes et naturelles.
 Consacree a l'etude et la propagation de la doctrine magnetique appliquee a la therapeutique, a la demonstration de l'immortalite de l'ame et au developpment de nos facultes naturelles, a la refutation de certaines croyances et de certains prejuges populaires, a la consecration de principe de la solidarite universelle, etc.  Psycologie et physiologie de la vie universelle, publiee avec l'appropbation du le concours de plusieurs docteurs en medicine, avocats, theologiens, savants, literateurs, magnetiseurs, mediums, et de simple croyants, etc., etc.  Cartomancie - Necromancie - Chiromancie -et Autres Sciences Mysterieuses Devoilees par la Pratique du Magnetisme.
 Experimentez et vous croirez!
 1859-1864?  Weekly
 Nimes, France.  Language:  French.  Publisher: L. Hachette, J.-B. Ballière, E. Dentu, M. Salles.  Editor: Manlius Salles.
 1/1, 1859.  16 pp.  12 francs/year in France.  Undated issues.  Noted in Auguste Viatte, "Origines françaises du spiritisme," in Revue de histoire de l'eglise de France, 1935.  Le Magnetiseur of Geneva in 1864 says that Salles, who ran a bookstore in Nimes, on his return to Nimes was to take up again the publication of the journal, which had been suspended.  The journal concentrated on magnetic healing and the new spiritism, but made space for some traditional elements of the occult sciences (in the light of magnetism) and for a discussion of the marvels of D.D. Home and an interview with the visiting American medium Squire.  BNF.
