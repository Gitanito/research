 	Rivista di Studi Psichici.
 Periodico Dedicato alle Ricerche Sperimentali e Critiche sui Fenomeni di Telepatia, Telestesia, Premonizione, Medianita, etc.
 1895–1900  Monthly
 Padua, then Turin, Italy, then Paris, France.  Language:  Italian.  Publisher: Tip. die fratelli Gallina.  Editor: Giambattista B. Ermacora and Giorgio Finzi, founders and editors; after 1898: Cesar Baudi de Vesme.  Succeeded by: Annales des Sciences Psychiques
 1/1, January 1895 -- 6/11-12, November-December 1900.  32 pp.

  When Ermacora was murdered in 1898, Cesar Baudi de Vesme (1862-  ) assumed the editorship, soon adding a French version of the journal, Revue des Etudes Psychiques.  Both were then merged into the Annales des Sciences Psychiques.  This was a very prominent journal of psychical research, with articles by all of the leading workers in the field, including, in December 1899, the first article by Ernesto Bozzano -- on spiritualism and scientific criticism.   The journal contained articles on exteriorization of sensibility, hypnotism, "effluviography," premonitions, the astral body, magic, magnetism, the phenomena of spiritualism, occultism, dreams, auto-suggestion, telepathy, Theosophy, etc.  Crabtree 1390.
