 Espiritismo, El.
 Revista Quincenal Dedicada a la Propagacion de la Doctrina Espiritista.
 1869-1878  Semimonthly
 Seville, Spain.  Language:  Spanish.  Publisher: Francisco Marti Bonneval, founder and director: El Circulo Liberal; Ipografico de Ariza y Ruiz; Imp. y Libreria Hijos de Fe.  Editor: Francisco Marti Bonneval, Jose Gomez, Juan Bautista Cano.
 Corporate author:  Sociedad Espiritista Sevillana
 1/1, March 1, 1869-August 28, 1878.  12-32 pp., 5 reals a quarter in Seville, and 6 in the provinces. 
  The third Spanish spiritualist journal and one of the best written.  The journal was suppressed by the authorities for eight issues in 1875.  This was an orthodox Kardecist journal, with a regular "Seccion Doctrinal" and excerpts from Kardec, and also published accounts of the meetings of the Sociedad Espiritista Sevillana, poetry and "Variedades."  Francisco Marti Bonneval, the founder and patron of the journal, died in 1876, and it was continued by his widow thereafter with the aid of Cano until It was suppressed by the authorities.  See Mario Mendez Bejarano, Historia de Filosofia en Espana hasta el Siglo XX (1927), section xiv; El Espiritism, online at http://filosofia.org/aut/mmb/hfe1714.htm; and Oscar M. Garcia Rodriguez, "Origenes del Periodismo Espirita en Espana," online at http://www.espiritismo.cc; Rodríguez, “Hemerografia Espiritista Espanola, 1857-1936,” https://grupoespiritaisladelapalma.wordpress.com.
