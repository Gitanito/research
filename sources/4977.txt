 Revue Mensuelle Religieuse, Politique, Scientifique.
 Complement de / faisant suite à la publication: Le Diable au XIXe siecle.
 1894--1897  Monthly
 Paris, France.  Language:  French. 
 Editor: Dr. Bataille (Dr. Charles Hacks and "Leo Taxil").
 Succeeds: Le Diable au XIXe Siecle  Succeeded by: La France Chretienne Antimaconnique (merged into)
 No. 1, January 1894-no. 37, January 1897.

 Published originally as a supplement to Le Diable au XIXe Siecle, which had been  published in 24 fascicles, beginning in November 1892 and then in two volumes in 1894.  This first revealed--supported with a vast repertoire of spurious documentation and an impressive knowledge of contemporary Masonry and occultism--the existence of the Satanist Palladian Order, once controlled by Albert Pike, that ruled Freemasonry.   "Dr. Bataille" was the collective pseudonym of Dr. Charles Hacks  and "Leo Taxil" (Gabriel-Antoine Jogand-Pages, 1857-1907.  This was merged into La France Chretienne Antimaconnique, which Taxil had started earlier and which was then being edited by his successor, A. Clarin de la Rive.  On Taxil, see the notes under La France Chretienne and Memoires d’une Ex-Palladiste.  Dorbon 4097.  BNF microfiche.
