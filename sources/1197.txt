THE LIBRARY.
The Society's Library of books and periodicals is open to Members..
and Associates alike, for consultation in the Society's Offices.
Members have the additional privilege of taking outbooks, except those
marked R, which are not to leave the rooms, according to the following
Rules:-
1. Members may obtain books either by writing to the Assistant-
Secretary, or by taking them from the shelves, in which latter
case they must leave a written acknowledgment on the form
provided for the purpose, entering also anybookswhiclithey may
at the same time return. l.Iembers must pay for the carriage of'
--, books sent to them or returned by them.
2. No Member may have more than two volumes out at once, or
retain any volume more than three weeks, without special
permission from the Assistant-Secretary. In the case of'
Members of ·Committees engaged on work for the Society·in
which the books are needed, this Rule will be relaxed. .
Further Rules will be added if occasion arises, but it is hoped that
Members will co-operate in taking care of the books, and returning
them promptly for the use of others. Many of the books are rare, and
would be hard to replace if lost.
A suggestion book is kept in the Society's Rooms, where names of'
books recommended- for purchase may be entered by Members and
Associates; or suggestions may be sent in writing to the Assistant-
Secretary. AI!y such recommendations will be carefully considered. by
the Library Committee.
By far the larger number of the existing books have been acquired by
gift. The few books purchased with the Society's funds have been, for-
the most part, of a distinctly scientific character.
