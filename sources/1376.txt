 Archiv fur den thierischen Magnetismus.
 In Verbindung mit mehreren Naturforschern.
 1817--1824  Three issues a year
 Altenburg and Leipzig, then Halle, then Leipzig, Germany. 
 Language:  German.
 Editor: Adolph Carl August Eschenmayer, Dieter  Georg Kieser, Christian Friedrich Nasse, Christian Gottfried Nees von Esenbeck.
 Publisher: F.A. Brockhaus; Hemmerde und Schwetschte. 
 Succeeded by: Sphinx: neues Archiv fur den thierischen Magnetismus (1825)
  1/1, 1817-1824.  200 pp. (varies). 
 Illustrated.

 Eschenmayer (1768-1852) was the chief mover behind this journal, assisted by Kieser (1779-1862), Nasse (1778-1851), and von Esenbeck (1776-1858).  Kieser went on to publish Sphinx, this journal's successor.  On von Esenbeck, who arranged the translation of A.J. Davis's works into German, see the note under Bibliothek des Spiritualismus fur Deutschland.  Eschenmayer was professor of philosophy and medicine at Tubingen, a friend of Justinus Kerner, and the first of the prominent Romantic parapsychologists.  This group of "pneumatologists" around the Archiv fur thierischen Magnetismus was fascinated with Schelling's philosophy and the "Night Side of Nature"--the Romantic shadow of the Enlightenment.  On the pneumatologists, see  C. Kiesewetter, Geschichte des neueren Occultismus (1891),  326-414.  The journal carried theoretical discussions of magnetism and its relationship with human magnetism and of clairvoyance, dreams, prophecies, the human will, rhabdomancy, second sight, dowsing, pendulums, varieties of "baguets" and their construction, magnetic prophetic dreams, amulettes, etc., case studies by physicians and others on healing, replies to controversies and defenses of magnetism, and notices of new literature on the subject.  Crabtree 269.  NY Academy of Medicine; NY State Library; Yale University; Harvard University; Johns Hopkins University; University of Texas, Austin; CrabIDS Basel Bern; LOC; etc.
