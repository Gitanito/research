    Summary:&nbsp;  From Pat Deveney's database:

 Gleaner, The
 A Magazine Devoted to the Science of Practical Christianity / A Magazine Devoted to the Study of Truth and its Application to the Needs of the Individual.
 The New Thought of Man.  The Larger Thought of God
 Other titles: W. John Murray's Magazine
 1911-1927  Monthly
 New York, NY.  Publisher: The Church of the Healing Christ; Divine Science Publishing Company.  Editor: Rev. W. John Murray, founder and editor.
 1/1, 1911-1927.  32 pp., $1.50 a year.

  Murray (1865-1925) was a disciple of Nona Brooks' Divine Science and the founder of the Church of the Healing Christ in New York (which held weekly services in the Waldorf-Astoria Hotel in Manhattan).  He was succeeded in the pulpit by Harry Gaze and then Emmet Fox.  Noted in The American Rosae Crucis, 1916, in the exchanges of The Master Mind, 1918, and in William C. Hartmann's Who's Who in Occult, Psychic and Spiritual Realms (1925).  NYPL; Wilmington University; University of Massachusetts, Amherst; McGill University; St. Andrews University.
