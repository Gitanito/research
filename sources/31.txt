   Journal of Practical Metaphysics, The.
 Devoted to the Unification of Scientific and Spiritual Thought and the New Philosophy of Health.
 1896-1898  Monthly
 Boston, MA.  Publisher: Philosophical Publishing Company.  Editor: Horatio Willis Dresser.  Succeeded by: The Arena (December 1898)
 Corporate author:  Metaphysical Club of Boston
1/1, October 1896-3/1, November 1898.  $1.50 a year, 10 cents a copy.

This was the journal of the Metaphysical Club of Boston, founded in 1895, the first association devoted to New Thought properly so called.  It was merged into The Arena.  Noted in the literature review in Neue Metapsychische Rundschau, June-July 1908.  Horatio Willis Dresser (1866-1954) was the son of Annetta Gertrude Dresser and Julius Dresser, who were among the first practitioners of what would become New Thought, as their son was its preeminent historian.  LOC; Yale University; Tulane University; Harvard University; University of Texas, Austin; NYPL.
