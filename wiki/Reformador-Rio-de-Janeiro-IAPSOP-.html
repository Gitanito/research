    Periodical: Reformador (Rio de Janeiro)

Summary:  From Pat Deveney's database:

Reformador.
Orgao Religioso de Espiritismo Cristao.
Deus, Cristo e Caridade
1882 Semimonthly, monthly, then semimonthly
Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Language: Portuguese. Editor: Agusto Elias da Silva; Louis Barreto. Corporate author: Orgao Religioso de Espiritismo Cristao, Federacao Espirita Brasileira
1/1, January 21, 1883-current 4-20pp.

The dominant journal in Brazil. Originally published from da Silva's house with a circulation of about 300. See "Revista 'Reformador' un Prodigioso Manantial de Paz y Amor," http://jorgeluizhessen.blogspot.com/2009/09/revista-Reformador-un-prodigioso.html. The Carrier Dove for May 1887 notes that the journal was four years old and was the organ of the Brazilian Spiritual Federation. The journal is listed as adhering to the 1892 Spanish-American spiritualist congress convened by Fraternidad Universal in Madrid to honor the discovery of America. In mid-1907 the journal featured a long attack on Alberto de Sarak, Count of Das. 2003 was the journal's 121st year. LOC microfilm.
