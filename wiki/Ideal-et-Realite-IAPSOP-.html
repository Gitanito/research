    Periodical: Ideal et Realite

Summary:  From Pat Deveney's database:

Ideal et Realite.
Litterature, Pensee, Art / Organe de l'Ecole Initiatique et du Groupe Ideal et Realite.
1922—1930 Bimonthly, monthly (10 issues a year)
Paris, France. Language: French. Publisher: editions du Faune; Publications I.R.
Editor: L.M. Themanlys (Louis Moyse), founder and editor; Eugène Blot; Paschal Themanlys; Ernest Marx; Gustave Rouger; Maurice Heim; S. Civet.
Succeeds: Le Mouvement Cosmique; Art, Science et Peuple
Corporate author: Organe de l'ecole Initiatique et du Groupe Ideal et Realite
1/1, May 5, 1922-7/55, September-December, 1930. 4-25 francs a year, 36-48 pp.

Louis Themanlys was a disciple of Max Theon, the founder of the Mouvement Cosmique, on whom see the note under Revue Cosmique, and the journal expounded especially the aesthetic (Symbolist) side of the movement in relation to the art, literature and theater of the period. Contributions by Claire Themanlys, Marc Semenoff, Jacques Janin, Paul Nicollet, Jacques Blot, Rene Callie, Louis Bouchet, Louis Berthaud, Maurice Ben Haroche, Pierre Lichtenberger, et al. Notable articles on "L'Elixir de Longue Vie," "La Panacee Universelle," and "La Fontaine de Jouvence" with especial attention there and elsewhere to Dr. Serge Voronoff, famous for his life-extending "greffes testiculaires." Curiously, the journal devoted little space to Theon. Noted in Astrosophie, January 1929. BNF; BM Lyon; NYPL.
