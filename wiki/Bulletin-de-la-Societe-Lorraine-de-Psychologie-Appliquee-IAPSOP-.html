Summary:  From Pat Deveney's database:

Bulletin de la Societe Lorraine de psychologie applique.
Suggestion, AutoSuggestion, Psychologie.
1913–1940 Semi-annual, quarterly
Jarville, then Nancy, France. Language: French. Publisher: Ars Graphiques Modernes. Editor: Emile Coue; Mme E. Coue.
No. 1, 1913-No. 74, 1940. 20 pp., 10 francs a year for membership in the society, which included a subscription for the journal.

The journal announced its goal as "the study of the phenomena due to Suggestion and the possible applications of these phenomena to education, re-education, cure of the sick, etc.” More specifically, it was devoted, with some academic pretensions in its later years, to the wonders of Emile Coue's teachings on self-improvement through autoSuggestion and the repetition of his mantra, "Every day, in every way, I'm getting better and better.” Noted in Hartmann's Who's Who in Occult, Psychic and Spiritual Realms (Jamaica, NY: Occult Press, 1925). BNF.
