    Periodical: Journal du Magnetisme

Summary:  From Pat Deveney's database:

Journal du Magnetisme.
Other titles: Journal du Magnetisme, du Massage et de la Psychologie
1845--1861 Semimonthly
Paris, France. Language: French. Publisher: A. Rene. Editor: Redige par une Societe de magnetiseurs et medecins, sous la direction de M. le Baron Dupotet de Sennevoy.
Succeeds: Journal de l’Ame (Geneva)? Succeeded by: Chaîne Magnetique; Revue de Magnetisme (1878)-->Journal du Magnetisme (1879)
Corporate author: Organe de la Societe magnetique de France / Organe de la Societe psychique internationale
1/1, 1845-1861 48-64 pp. a month. From internal evidence the journal appears to have been published in semimonthly fascicles.

Baron Jules Denis Du Potet (or Dupotet) de Sennevoy (1796-1881) founded the journal and edited the first series (until 1861). The journal was revived in 1878 by by Hector Durville as Revue Magnetique and the name changed the next year to the original, and also continued as La Chaine Magnetique by Dupotet himself. Dupotet was extraordinarily important in the development of occultism in the nineteenth century both through this journal and through his La Magie Devoilee, ou Principes de Science Occulte (1852) which forcefully identified the principles active in mesmerism with those employed in traditional magic. The journal was riven in its later years by the fight between those who insisted on the magnetic "fluid" as the cause of the then-current phenomena, and those who, at least, wanted to include the spirits of the dead as movers. The Crabtree 518. ZDB: Freiburg Inst Grenzgeb Psychol.; BL; Harvard University
