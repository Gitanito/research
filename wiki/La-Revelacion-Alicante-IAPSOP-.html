Revelacion, La.
Revista Espiritista Alicantina / Revista Espiritista.
1872 Semimonthly, then weekly, semimonthly; monthly in 1874; currently bimonthly
Alicante, then (January 1905)
Barcelona, Spain.
Language: Spanish.
Publisher: Vincente Costa y Cia.; Imprenta de Moscat y Onate (1900 on).
Editor: Dr. Manuel Auso y Monzo (1814-1891); Miguel Gimeno Eito; Angel Aguarod.
Corporate author: Sociedad Alicantina de Estudios Psicologicos / Organo Oficial de la Federacion Espiritista Valenciana / Afiliada a la Union Espiritista Kardeciana de Cataluna 1/1, January 5, 1872-current. 12-24 pp., varies. 16 reals a year in Alicante, 20 in the rest of Spain.

The journal’s primary interest was in its "Seccion Doctrinal," devoted to lengthy expositions of the Kardecist doctrine. Auso y Monzo, a prominent homeopathic doctor, and a republican and Mason, had been the first to import Kardec’s The Book of Spirits into Spain and then began the Socidedad Progreso Espiritista in Alicante, of which this journal became the organ. The first issues examined the work of the medium Juan Perez in producing spirit photographs, a real novelty in Spain at the time. The journal published the spiritualist poet Amália Domingo y Soler (1835-1909), who had formally become a spiritist in 1875 and who then went to edit La Luz del Porvenir while continuing to submit articles to this journal. Throughout 1899 the journal reviewed the press reviews of the spiritualist plays of Miguel Gimeno Eito, then editor of the journal, which the journal published. A journal of the same name was started in the early 1970s after a hiatus from at least the late 1930s. Biblioteca Nacional de Espana; Archivo General de la Guerra Civil Española; Biblioteca Nacional de Espana.
