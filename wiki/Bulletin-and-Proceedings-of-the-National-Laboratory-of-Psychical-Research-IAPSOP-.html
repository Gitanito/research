The Proceedings of the National Laboratory and its successor, The Bulletin, were the companions of the British Journal of Psychical Research, intended to record the Society's practical experiments. Part 1 of volume 1 of the Proceedings was published in January 1927 and devoted to the telekinetic phenomena of Eleonore Zugen, and part 2 of that volume was published in April 1929, with Harry Price's "Short-Title Catalogue" of the Society. The volumes were replete with plates of etchings and drawings in the Society's collections and with photographs of its experiments. The subjects studied included Rudi Schneider, Mrs. Duncan, and others. British Library; University of Mississippi; University of Missouri; University of Virginia; and many others.
