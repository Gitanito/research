Estrella, La.
Revista Mensual Internacional / Edicion Espanola de The Star.
Other titles: Boletin Internacional de La Estrella (September 1929); Revista de la Estrella (1931-1933)
1928—1933 Bimonthly, monthly
Madrid, Spain. Language: Spanish.
Publisher: Star Publishing Trust/Editora de La Estrella.
Editor: Emily Lutjens, D. Rajagopal; Francisco Rovirain Spain.
Succeeded by: Boletiín Internacional de La Estrella (September 1929)—>Revista de la Estrella (1931-1933) 1/1, March 1928. 24 pp., 6 pesetas in Spain, 10 elsewhere.

This began as the Spanish-language version of the International Star Bulletin published in Ommen, Holland, under the direction of Emily Lutyens and D. Rajagopal and others after Annie Besant in January prematurely proclaimed that the World Teacher (Jiddu Krishnamurti) had come.

After Krishnamurti renounced his supposed messiahship and dissolved the Order of the Star in August 1929, the Spanish version became in September 1929 the Boletin Internacional de La Estrella and then, in January 1931 Revista de la Estrella, al devoted to the continuing cult of Krishnamurti. Archivo General de la Guerra Espanola.

Revista de la Estrella.
1931—1933 Monthly
Madrid, Spain. Editor: Francisco Rovira.
1/1, January 1931-August 1933. 24-36 pp., 10-8 pesetas in Spain.

This was the successor of the various journals of the Order of The Star and included poems and conferences of J. Krishnamurti and articles by Claude Bragdon and others. BNE.
